Research

Video Object Detection

In this ongoing project, we analyze object proposals in video frames and aim for segmenting and detecting video objects in temporally consistent way.

For video object detection, I am exploring CNN features and using CAFFE deep learning toolbox. I am analyzing the domain shift issues and fine-tuning CAFFE reference model and improving object detection in video.

Semantic segmentation - multiclass labeling

We explored multiclass image-sequence segmentation. We have evaluated several multiclass image segmentation methods and looked at improving video multiclass segmentation.

We implemented our framework for dense-CRF based multiclass video segmentation with improved accuracy compared to multiclass image segmentation with no additional time overhead. We are exploring on object detection and deep-learning based object recognition.

Source Code can be found in GitHub

StreamGBH+ : Improved streaming hierarchical video segmentation

In this project, we segment a video in hierarchical streaming fashion. We augment streamGBH by University at Buffalo with bilateral filtering and motion feature and we achieve significant improvement in segmnetation quality and accuracy. We call this approach as streamGBH+. Our work on streamGBH+ has been accepted for publication in WACV 2014 .

Hierarchical Motion layer Segmentation

We proposed a online hierarchical motion layer based segmentation framework. We begin with oversegmentations and group them iteratively in layers of hierarchies based on geometric distances between the regions. The oversegmentations can be in the form of image blocks, or superpixels or any other segmentations. The geometric distance has been evaluated in the form of directed divergence. Our work on online hierarchical motion segmentation framework has been accepted for publication in WACV 2014 .
